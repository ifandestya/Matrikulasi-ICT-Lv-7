Chapter 1

Computer Basics & Good Posture

🧍 Sitting Correctly at the Computer

Did you know that sitting in the wrong position can hurt your eyes, back, and neck? Here are the golden rules to stay healthy while using a computer!

  • 👁️ Eyes: The monitor should be 40–75 cm from your eyes — about the length of your arm. Use a screen filter to reduce eye strain and blink often!
  • 🏃 Light: Don't let light shine directly into your eyes. Sit facing away from windows if you can.
  • 💪 Elbows: Keep your elbows at the same height as the keyboard so your arms don't get tired.
  • 🪑 Back & neck: Sit straight! Use a chair with a backrest. Your head and neck should be in line with your body — no hunching!
  • ⏰ Rest: Take a break every 20 minutes. Look far away and stretch a little.

🔧 How to Take Care of Your Laptop

A laptop is a tool — treat it well and it will last a long time! Here are the most important tips:

⚠️

Don't do these!

Don't use your laptop on a bed or pillow — heat can't escape and it will overheat! Don't put heavy things on top of it either.

✅

Good habits

Charge your laptop when it reaches 20%. Unplug when it hits 100%. This keeps the battery healthy longer!

⚡ Turning Your Computer On & Off

💡

The 4 Power Options — What's the difference?

Shutdown = Everything is off. Good for long breaks. You'll need to wait for boot-up next time.
Sleep = Computer is resting but RAM is still on. Wake it up in seconds!
Restart = Shuts down and starts again. Use this when something is not working right.
Hibernate = Like sleep, but saves everything to the hard drive. No electricity used. Best of both worlds!

✅ Steps to turn ON a desktop computer:

  1. Plug the computer into the power outlet.
  2. Make sure the keyboard and mouse are connected.
  3. Turn on the monitor.
  4. Press the Power button on the CPU case.
  5. Wait for the computer to finish starting up (booting).
  6. Your computer is now ready to use! 🎉

✅ How to properly SHUT DOWN a laptop:

  1. Close all open apps and save your work.
  2. Remove any USB drives or flash disks.
  3. Press the Windows key or Ctrl+Alt+Del.
  4. Click the Power menu and choose Shut Down.
  5. Press Enter and wait. Your laptop will turn off safely. ✅

Chapter 2

File Management

📂 What is File Management?

File management means organizing your files and folders on a computer — like keeping your bedroom tidy, but digitally! You can create folders, rename them, move files, and delete things you don't need.

🗂️ Creating a New Folder:

💻

Method 1 – Top menu

Open Windows Explorer → Click New Folder in the Home tab → Type the name you want.

🖱️

Method 2 – Right click

Right-click on an empty space → Choose New → Folder → Type the folder name.

✏️ Renaming, Moving & Deleting:

  • ✏️Rename: Right-click the file/folder → choose Rename → type the new name. Or double-click slowly on the name.
  • ✂️Move: Drag and drop to a new folder, OR right-click → Cut (Ctrl+X) → go to destination → Paste (Ctrl+V).
  • 🗑️Delete: Right-click → Delete. Files go to the Recycle Bin first. Right-click in Recycle Bin and choose Restore if you made a mistake!

📦 ZIP and RAR Files — Compressed Files!

Sometimes files are very big and take a long time to send. We can compress them — like squishing a pillow into a bag — to make them smaller!

🗜️

Compress to ZIP

Right-click the folder → Send to → Compressed (zipped) folder. Done!

📦

Compress to RAR

Right-click the folder → Add to "[folder name].rar" (requires WinRAR installed). RAR can make files up to 40% smaller!

Mouse, Keyboard & Touch Typing

🖱️ Using a Mouse

The mouse is an input device — it tells the computer what you want to do by moving a pointer on the screen.

  • 👆Left Click: Opens programs, selects items, clicks buttons. Most used!
  • 👆Right Click: Shows a menu of options (like right-clicking on your desktop to change the wallpaper).
  • 👆👆Double Click: Two quick clicks — opens apps and files.
  • 🤚Drag and Drop: Hold the left button and move the mouse to drag an icon or file to a new spot, then release.

Web Browser

🌐 What is a Web Browser?

A web browser is software that lets you visit websites on the internet. Think of it like a magic window 🪟 that shows you the whole internet!

🤔

Browser vs. Search Engine — What's the difference?

Browser = the app you use (like Google Chrome, Firefox). It's like a car 🚗.
Search Engine = the service that finds websites (like Google Search, Bing). It's like a road map 🗺️.
You use the car to follow the map!

✨ What can a browser do for you?

  • 🔍Search for information: Type what you need and find it instantly!
  • ⭐Save your favorite pages: Use the bookmark feature so you can go back without remembering the address.
  • 🔒Protect you from bad websites: The browser checks if a website is safe before you visit it.
  • 🖥️Show web pages correctly: Websites are written in code (HTML, CSS, JavaScript). The browser translates it into text, images, and videos you can see.
  • 🔧Support search engines: Google, Bing, and DuckDuckGo work through your browser to find pages.

Chapter 5

Email

📧 What is Email?

Email (Electronic Mail) is like a digital letter 📬. You can send messages, documents, photos, and even videos — instantly — to anyone in the world! You just need their email address.

📋 3 Main Uses of Email:

  • 💬Communication: Send messages with text, pictures, documents, and videos to friends, family, or teachers.
  • 📢Promotions: Businesses use email to tell you about new products or sales (you've probably seen these in your inbox!).
  • 🔔Notifications & reminders: Get notified about deadlines, meetings, or updates.

⚖️ Good Things & Bad Things About Email

✅ The Good Stuff

  • Free to use 🆓
  • Can attach documents & photos
  • Send to many people at once
  • Eco-friendly — no paper! 🌱
  • Works on phone, tablet, laptop
  • Organized with folders & labels

❌ Watch Out For…

  • SPAM — unwanted junk emails
  • Viruses hidden in attachments
  • Easy to send to the wrong person
  • No facial expressions or tone of voice
  • Phishing — fake emails that trick you!
🚨

Stay Safe Online!

Never open attachments from people you don't know. If an email asks for your password or money, it is almost always a SCAM. Ask a trusted adult! 🛡️

📤 How to Send an Email (Gmail)

  1. Open your browser and go to gmail.com.
  2. Log in with your Google account.
  3. Click the big "Compose" (Tulis) button at the top left.
  4. In the "To" field, type the recipient's email address.
  5. Add a Subject — this is like the title of your email.
  6. Write your message in the big box.
  7. Click the Send button. Done! 🎉
📌

Pro Tip: CC and BCC

CC (Carbon Copy) = Send a copy to someone else, and everyone can see who got it.
BCC (Blind Carbon Copy) = Same but the other recipients can't see who else got it. Great for privacy!

10-Finger Typing Mastery

🚀 Why Learn 10-Finger Typing?

Most people type with only 2 or 4 fingers — they look down at the keyboard and hunt for each key. Touch typing with all 10 fingers is completely different! You train your fingers to remember where every key is, so you never need to look down.

🐢 Hunt & Peck (2 fingers)

  • Look at keyboard constantly
  • Average: 20–30 WPM
  • Tiring and slow
  • Hard to improve further
  • Eyes jumping between screen & keys

🚀 Touch Typing (10 fingers)

  • Eyes stay on the screen
  • Average: 50–80 WPM when trained
  • Fast & efficient
  • Can reach 100+ WPM with practice
  • Hands work like a pianist's!
⚡

WPM = Words Per Minute

Average typist: 40 WPM · Good typist: 60–80 WPM · Professional: 80–100 WPM · World record: 216 WPM! 🏆 Your target for this year: 15+ WPM — you've got this!

🗺️ Which Finger Presses Which Key?

Feel the bumps on F and J!

Look at a real keyboard and feel with your index fingers — you'll find a small raised bump on F (left index) and J (right index). These are your homing guides. Without looking, place your fingers on these bumps and all your other fingers will be in the right position automatically!

✅ The 5 Golden Rules of Touch Typing

  1. Home Row First: Always return your fingers to ASDF (left) and JKL; (right) after pressing any key. Think of Home Row as your "base camp" — fingers always come back!
  2. Never Look Down: Cover the keyboard with a cloth if you have to! Looking up at the screen forces your brain to memorize key positions. It's uncomfortable at first — that's normal!
  3. Use the Correct Finger: Each key has an assigned finger (see the colour map above). Using the wrong finger is a bad habit. Correct habits now = fast typing later!
  4. Light Touch, Low Force: Don't slam the keys! Tap them lightly. This keeps your fingers relaxed and reduces mistakes and fatigue.
  5. Accuracy Before Speed: It's better to type slowly and correctly than fast and full of mistakes. Speed comes automatically as accuracy improves!
🎹

Typing is Like Playing Piano!

A pianist doesn't look at the keys while playing — their fingers know exactly where to go from memory. Touch typing works the same way. At first it feels impossible. After a few weeks of practice, it feels completely natural. Commit to 10 minutes of practice every day!
